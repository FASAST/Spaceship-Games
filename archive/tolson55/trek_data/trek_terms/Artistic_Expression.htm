Artistic Expression

This skill encompasses fine arts, performing arts, and applied arts. Training is a combination of guided practice in technique and instruction in theory and important contributions to the field. Training in any of the performing arts includes instruction in repertoire as well as extensive practice in solo and ensemble performance. The greater the skill in this area, the finer the expression of the art forms chosen.

This skill can be used by a character that desires to produce a piece of art or perform for non-player characters, such as for an important person on a newly discovered planet. A character attempting to reproduce a musical code, recognition sign, or even an alien language, for example, also may use skill in music.

A separate Skill Rating must be developed for each different type of art form. Typical choices in the fine arts are painting with oil, watercolor, or light, sculpting in stone, plastic, or gemstones, and writing short stories or poetry. Typical choices in the performing arts include drama, vocal or instrumental music (specifying the instrument), and such dance forms as jazz or zero-G ballet; typical choices in the applied arts include optical photography, holography, graphic design, and so on.
