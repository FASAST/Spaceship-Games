Star Clash - Independents Report
Days 2/2504.11 - 2/2504.20

Affiliation of Outer Free Worlds (AOFW)
2/2504.11 • A large force of Federation ships is detected by a listening post in the Hoot system heading "south" from the area of Flitner. Morning Garden picks up the force. It leaves sensor range in the area of the Geisling system.
A small force of Klingon warp signatures is detected following the Federation force. Intelligence estimates believe this is a group of IKS ships.
2/2504.13 • Two small forces of Federation ships are detected by a listening post in the Archibald system heading "south". Over the course of the next few days the Fountainworld and Paxton systems also detect the forces. The Federation forces leave detection range on 2/2504.20.
2/2504.14 • A request is received through the Star Fleet liaison office to allow a group of damaged Star Fleet ships to be repaired at Baker's World. The request is granted.
2/2504.16 • Part of a unit known as Task Force 24 under Vice Admiral Kinneson enters the AOFW near the Devotion system. The unit consists of a battleship, two cruisers, and two frigates. It is met by the gunboat Lockhart which escorts it to Baker's World.
2/2504.18 • The Federation force arrives at Baker's World. They are told that the High Haven outpost will be available for their use starting on 2/2504.21.

Independent Worlds
2/2504.13 • Farkin - A Star Fleet force arrives at the system. The Pulsar class warpshuttles Rinbu and Nadsir meet the fleet part way into the system. The fleet is politely greeted but informed that warships are not allowed in the system. As is normal, the shuttles beam informative and spiritually uplifting literature to the closest ships. The Star Fleet ships immediately go onto the defensive. Their commander, Rear Admiral T'nek warns of "severe consequences" if there is a repeat of the incident. Despite their defensive efforts, the transporters on the two warpshuttles beat the defenses of the cruiser Dianna and the frigate Barnett. Upon investigating, the crews of these ships find their living quarters inundated with pamphlets for the Hari Krishna movement. Obviously not welcome, and not wanting to hang around, the force leaves the next day. Sensors also note that a small group of Klingon ships are following the Star Fleet ships.
• Farkin - The Star Fleet force in this system is engaged by a Klingon fleet which arrives from the "north". In the Battle of Farkin the Star Fleet force is routed and flees from the system to the "north".
2/2504.15 • Flood - A modest sized Star Fleet force arrives and joins the ships already in the system.
• Flood - Two hours later a Klingon force enters the outer system from the "east". The huge Star Fleet force moves to engage the Klingon force, however, the Klingons quietly leave the system at warp 6 headed "west".
2/2504.16 • Flood - The Star Fleet force leaves the system headed "east".
2/2504.17 • Deerlam - Star Fleet's Task Force 89 arrives at the system. The planetary government is pleased to see the fleet arrive, although expressing some reservations about a threat from the Romulans if the system was to be seen as a fleet location. Rear Admiral T'nek's, the fleet commander, offers ten days of joint training with the garrison. This is happily accepted. A group of three Klingon ships is noted entering the system well behind the Star Fleet force.
2/2504.18 • Deerlam - Task Force 89 turns to attack the Klingons. The Klingons run from the system rather than face the Federation fleet.
• Farkin - A huge Star Fleet force enters the system from the "west".
2/2504.19 • Deerlam - Star Fleet's Task Force 89 begins joint training with the system's garrison. The Klingon ships return to the outer edge of the system and just sit there.
2/2504.20 • Currie - Two Star Fleet destroyers, the Aragor and Singles arrive in the system. The planetary governor notes the arrival of the reinforcements he requested when Task Force 89 visited, and is pleased. He sends a very nice thank you note to Star Fleet Triangle Sector Headquarters. The captains of the ships, seem somewhat befuddled; but they smile and nod politely for the cameras.
